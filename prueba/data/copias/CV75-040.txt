The rise of autonomous vehicles (AVs) heralds a transformative era in transportation, driven by the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and learning algorithms. This paper comprehensively explores AI's evolution within AVs, tracing its journey from foundational principles to recent advancements. It delves into AI's role in shaping autonomous decision-making capabilities, addressing ethical considerations and bias in AI-driven software development. Statistical insights into AI usage within the automotive industry are presented, along with discussions on algorithm refinement and autonomy levels. Additionally, the paper examines software package sizes across autonomy levels, highlighting AI's nuanced usage and automation of key tasks.
