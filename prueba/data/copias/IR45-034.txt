Artificial intelligence stands as a burgeoning technology simulating human intelligence in machines, programmed to emulate human thought processes and actions. Autonomous vehicles epitomize this innovation, capable of operating and executing functions sans human intervention. This transformative technology holds promise for enhanced passenger safety, reduced traffic congestion, optimized traffic flow, decreased fuel consumption, minimized pollution, and enriched travel experiences. Beyond passenger transport, autonomous vehicles play pivotal roles across industries spanning agriculture, transportation, and military applications. These vehicles' operations hinge on sensor data and a subset of artificial intelligence systems. Artificial intelligence encompasses data collection, path planning, and execution in autonomous vehicles, necessitating machine learning techniques intrinsic to artificial intelligence. However, privacy concerns and security vulnerabilities accompany these advancements. This article explores cybersecurity challenges associated with integrating artificial intelligence into autonomous vehicles, contextualized within the evolving landscape of self-driving automobiles.
