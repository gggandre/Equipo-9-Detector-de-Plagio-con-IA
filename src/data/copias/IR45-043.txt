This study delves into human-chatbot interactions among computer science students at Uppsala University, Sweden, to discern the empathy dynamics therein. Through qualitative data collection via semi-structured interviews with five students, this study unveils nuanced perceptions regarding anthropomorphic chatbots as either machines or humans, alongside the prevalence of verbal abuse during such interactions. Additionally, gender dynamics influence participants' expectations of chatbot helpfulness, reflecting subtle nuances in human-chatbot empathy interactions.
